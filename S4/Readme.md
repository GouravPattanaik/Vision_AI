<h4><a id="user-content-here-is-the-step-by-step-approach-being-taken-to-achieve-the-required-goals-meeting-994-accuracy-under-the-required-parameters-20k-and-within-20-epochs-" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#here-is-the-step-by-step-approach-being-taken-to-achieve-the-required-goals-meeting-994-accuracy-under-the-required-parameters-20k-and-within-20-epochs-"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Here is the step-by-step approach, being taken to achieve the required goals (meeting 99.4%+ accuracy, under the required parameters: &lt;=20K and within 20 epochs) :</h4>
<p>Fix up the required architecture/layer-organization...in terms of choosing the number of kernels for each layer, am trying to follow a 'multiple-of-8' numbers (from 8, 16, 24, 32...etc):</p>
<p>Architecture will have 3 "components":</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>One initial,"image-conv2d" layer at the begining, to convolve over the "original image" channels, am
initially providing 8 number of kernels for this 'separate' layer (which feeds in to the next<br>
"2-conv2d-layer-block").It needs to be noted that this 1 initial layer + The 2 following layers(for
"2-conv2d-layer-block") provide receptive field of 7x7 pixels(3-&gt;5-&gt;7) sufficient for the MNIST dataset's
edges &amp; gradient generation.In this evolution-experiment, kernel numbers started out as 8 initially,
but in the final architecture(which met the requirements) it became 16.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>conv2d-BLOCK with 2 layers (in this case):
This block will be placed after the first "image-conv2d" layer, and one more instance of this block, will
also follow the transition-block (explained below) later.
In this evolution-experiment, kernel numbers initially started out as (8-16) for the 'first-2-layer-block'
&amp; (8-16) for the 'second-2-layer-block', but in the final architecture(which met the requirements) it
became (16-16) for the 'first-2-layer-block' &amp; (24-24) for the 'second-2-layer-block'.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Transition Blocks:
1st transition layer, with both max-pool(k=2,s=2) and a-1x1-feature-merger kernel, following the
'first-2-layer-block'.
2nd transition layer, towards the end (following the 2nd conv2d-block) which does NOT have
the maxpool (i.e. just has 1x1-feature-merger operator), and followed by the Global
Average Pooling (GAP) layer leading upto the Softmax layer.<br>
Here, at the end, we have another 'organization-possibility' i.e. we can also have a GAP layer followed
by a 1x1 operator (which actually resembles a fully-connected(FC) layer in this case. (Note: for my
experiments, am finding that the 1x1, followed by GAP gave BETTER results, as compared to GAP followed
by 1x1(in a FC-way).
Hence, will be showing this evolution of incremental changes to 1x1-&gt;GAP organization rather than
GAP-&gt;1x1 (though, the first 2 networks(NW) below show both of them, but later iterations build upon
the basic-NW having '1x1-&gt;GAP' organization only)</p>
</li>
</ol>
